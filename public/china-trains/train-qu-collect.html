How to Collect Tickets?

* We offer 2 methods to get train tickets:
Pick up the booked train ticket by yourself and deliver train ticket to your hotel in China or private address in China.
* Pick up the Booked Train Tickets by yourself
What All the passengers’ original passports which used for booking and pick-up code provided by CTT
Where Online Booking Window of ticket office in the departure train station. 取票窗口、取票专口、网络订票
When You can pick up the ticket at any time after it is issued. If you have to collect it on  the day of departure before boarding. It is advised to arrive at the station at least 1-1.5 hours in advance. In the peak period, such as Chinese New Year, 1.5-2 hours in advance is better.

Notes:

Self-service ticket machines at the stations only can be used by China ID Card and cannot print online bookings for foreign passengers, so do ticket outlets outside the station.If you have booked multi-trip tickets, it’s better to collect them all at the first departure station to save time. The train station will collect CNY 5/per ticket as non-departure station service charge.Some stations also have English counters, which save trouble for foreign passengers. In Beijing West Railway Station, it is counter 16 at east ticket hall of north square.

Some useful Chinese sentences:

Hi, please take me to Train Station. 你好，请带我去火车站 (show ticket to the taxi driver).
Hi, I want to pick up my ticket. 你好，我要取票.
This is the pick-up code and my passport. 这是订单号和我的护照.
Thank you! 谢谢!
* Deliver train tickets to your hotel in China or private address in China
It is strongly recommended to use the ticket delivery service, you can get the paper tickets at your hotel in China or a private address in China. You won’t stand in the long queue in crowed train station and face the communication problem. The hotel concierge can collect the express for you. The express will arrive at the hotel on your check-in day or one day early. We will provide the tracking number to you which can help you to find your express from hotel.
